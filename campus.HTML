The Client Challenge

Validating a market opportunity in Nigeria's underserved student internet market

What the Client Needed

A startup had identified a potential opportunity: Nigerian university students were spending massive amounts on unreliable mobile data. But they needed answers to critical questions before launching:

  • Is the pain real? Are students actually frustrated enough to switch from mobile data to campus Wi-Fi?
  • Will students pay? Even if the service is cheaper, will they trust it and subscribe?
  • What's the financial model? Can this work at scale? What's the path to profitability?
  • How do we pitch investors? What story, data, and projections will secure funding?

They needed more than surface-level research. They needed deep market validation, defensible financial projections, and investor-ready materials that could withstand scrutiny.

The Stakes

High Risk: Building physical infrastructure (Starlink, solar power, routers) across multiple universities requires ‚Ç¶20-35M per campus upfront.

High Reward: If successful, this becomes a scalable recurring-revenue business with 1.8M potential customers and minimal churn (students need internet).

Why This Matters

This isn't just a business opportunity‚Äîit's about educational equity. Students from low-income backgrounds can't afford ‚Ç¶12,000-‚Ç¶20,000/month for data. Campus Wi-Fi at ‚Ç¶3,000-‚Ç¶5,500/month levels the playing field.
