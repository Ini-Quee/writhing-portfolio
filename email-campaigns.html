You've changed lives.

But you're making $4K/month. Working 50 hours/week.

One client cancels and your whole month is ruined.

You're a great COACH. But a terrible BUSINESS OWNER.

Nobody taught you business. They taught you nutrition, exercise science, behavior change, client psychology.

Group programs. Digital courses. Templates and systems. Community accountability.

These are all LEVERAGED. Meaning: You build them once. And sell them over and over. Without adding more hours to your week.

That's how you go from $4K/month to $40K/month. Not by working more. By working SMARTER.

Sample 5: The Commodity Problem

Creative Business Positioning
Subject: Why "charge what you're worth" doesn't work

[First Name],

$500 logo. That's what the client offered.

You know you're worth $5,000. But you take the $500 anyway.

Because: "At least it's something."

And you tell yourself: "Clients just don't want to pay premium prices."

Here's the truth:

It's not that they don't want to pay. It's that YOU haven't given them a reason to.

Here's what I mean:

When a client sees your portfolio and thinks: "This looks like everyone else's work."

They default to PRICE.

Because if all logos look the same, why pay $5K when someone else charges $500?

But when they see your work and think: "This is DIFFERENT. This is exactly what I need."

Price becomes irrelevant.

Because you're no longer a commodity. You're a SPECIALIST.

Most creatives think: "I just need better mindset around pricing."

No. You need better DIFFERENTIATION.

Because "charge what you're worth" only works when you're WORTH charging premium. And you're only worth charging premium when you're DIFFERENT.

Tomorrow I'll show you the exact framework: how to position yourself so premium pricing becomes inevitable.

Talk soon,
[Signature]

P.S. Charging premium prices when you're a commodity = delusion. Differentiate first. Price second.
